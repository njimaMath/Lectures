独立確率変数列に対する大数の法則と中心極限定理

Markov and Chebyshev inequalities, weak/strong LLN, and CLT via characteristic functions.

1. Markov inequality

Let $X$ be a random variable such that $\mathbb P(X\ge 0)=1$. Then

$$ \mathbb P(X\ge 1) \le \mathbb E[X]. $$
Proof

Since $\mathbf 1_{\{X\ge 1\}} \le X\mathbf 1_{\{X\ge 1\}} \le X$, we have

$$ \mathbb P(X\ge 1) = \mathbb E[\mathbf 1_{\{X\ge 1\}}] \le \mathbb E[X]. $$

2. Chebyshev's inequality

For any $t>0$,

$$ \mathbb P(|X-\mathbb E[X]|\ge t) \le \frac{\mathrm{Var}(X)}{t^2}. $$
Proof
$$ \mathbb P(|X-\mathbb E[X]|\ge t) = \mathbb P\left(\frac{|X-\mathbb E[X]|^2}{t^2}\ge 1\right) \le \frac{\mathbb E[(X-\mathbb E[X])^2]}{t^2} = \frac{\mathrm{Var}(X)}{t^2}. $$

This is Markov's inequality applied to $(X-\mathbb E[X])^2$.

3. Law of Large Numbers

Let

$$ \bar{X}_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n X_i. $$

Weak law

Let $X_1,\dots,X_n$ be i.i.d. with $\mathbb E[|X_1|]<\infty$ and $\mathrm{Var}(X_1)<\infty$. Then $\bar{X}_n \to \mathbb E[X_1]$ in probability.

Proof

For $\varepsilon>0$,

$$ \mathbb P(|\bar{X}_n-\mathbb E[X_1]|>\varepsilon) = \mathbb P(|\bar{X}_n-\mathbb E[\bar{X}_n]|>\varepsilon) \le \frac{\mathrm{Var}(\bar{X}_n)}{\varepsilon^2}. $$

Since $\mathrm{Var}(\bar{X}_n)=\frac{1}{n^2}\sum_{i=1}^n \mathrm{Var}(X_i) = \frac{\mathrm{Var}(X_1)}{n}$, the bound tends to $0$.

Strong law

Let $X_1,\dots,X_n$ be i.i.d. with $\mathbb E[X_1^4]<\infty$. Then $\bar{X}_n \to \mathbb E[X_1]$ almost surely.

Proof sketch (report)

There exists $C>0$ such that

$$ \mathbb E[(\bar{X}_n-\mathbb E[\bar{X}_n])^4] \le C n^{-2}. $$

Hence

$$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\mathbb E[(\bar{X}_n-\mathbb E[\bar{X}_n])^4] < \infty. $$

Therefore

$$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(\bar{X}_n-\mathbb E[\bar{X}_n])^4 < \infty \quad \text{a.s.} $$

Since $\sum |a_n|<\infty$ implies $a_n\to 0$, we conclude $\bar{X}_n-\mathbb E[\bar{X}_n] \to 0$ a.s.

4. Central Limit Theorem

Let $X_1,\dots,X_n$ be i.i.d. with $m=\mathbb E[X_1]$ and $v=\mathrm{Var}(X_1)<\infty$. Then

$$ Y_n := \sqrt{n}(\bar{X}_n - m) \Rightarrow N(0,v). $$

Lemma (report)

$$ a_n \to a \in \mathbb R \quad \Longrightarrow \quad \left(1-\frac{a_n}{n}\right)^n \to e^{-a}. $$
Proof sketch via characteristic functions

Recall that $Y_n \Rightarrow N(0,v)$ iff $\phi_{Y_n}(\xi)\to e^{-v\xi^2/2}$. Moreover,

$$ \phi_{Y_n}(\xi)=\mathbb E\left[\exp\left(\frac{i\xi}{\sqrt{n}} \sum_{k=1}^n (X_k-m)\right)\right] = \left(\mathbb E\left[e^{\frac{i\xi}{\sqrt{n}}(X_1-m)}\right]\right)^n. $$

Using the expansion

$$ e^x = 1 + x + \frac{x^2}{2} + \frac{1}{2}\int_0^1 x^3(1-t)^2 e^{tx}\,dt, $$

and $\mathbb E[X_1-m]=0$, we obtain

$$ \mathbb E\left[e^{\frac{i\xi}{\sqrt{n}}(X_1-m)}\right] = 1 - \frac{\xi^2}{2n}\int_0^1 \mathbb E\left[(X_1-m)^2 e^{\frac{i\xi t}{\sqrt{n}}(X_1-m)}\right]dt. $$

By dominated convergence, the integral tends to $\mathbb E[(X_1-m)^2]=v$, hence

$$ \mathbb E\left[e^{\frac{i\xi}{\sqrt{n}}(X_1-m)}\right] = 1-\frac{v\xi^2}{2n}+o\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right). $$

Applying the lemma yields $\phi_{Y_n}(\xi)\to e^{-v\xi^2/2}$.
